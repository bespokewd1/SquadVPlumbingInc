Know When It Needs Service

Sump pump problems are easier to control before the pit overflows. These are the calls we want homeowners and property managers to make early.

  1. Urgent

    Water after rain or snowmelt

    Standing water, damp carpet, or a pit that cannot keep up can mean the pump is blocked, undersized, or failing under load.

  2. Check

    Pump will not start, stop, or drain properly

    Float switches, check valves, discharge lines, power supply, and worn motors all need a clear inspection before the next storm.

  3. Plan

    No backup for outages or spring melt

    A battery backup system can keep water moving when heavy weather and power loss happen at the same time.

What We Check Before Recommending a Fix

We do not treat every sump pump call like a simple swap. The right answer depends on the pit, float, valve, discharge route, power setup, and how much water the property has to manage.

  • Pump operation and motor condition
  • Sump pit condition and water level
  • Float switch movement
  • Check valve performance
  • Discharge line routing
  • Battery backup options
