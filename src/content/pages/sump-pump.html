The Problem

Common Sump Pump Problems

Small pump issues can become expensive water damage fast. Call when you notice the warning signs.

Water After Rain or Snowmelt

If water is collecting around the basement, the pump may be undersized, blocked, or no longer moving water out properly.

Pump Will Not Turn On

A failed float switch, power issue, stuck pump, or burned-out motor can leave your basement unprotected.

Runs Too Often

Constant cycling can point to a drainage issue, check valve problem, or a pump working harder than it should.

Strange Noise or Vibration

Grinding, rattling, or heavy vibration can mean the pump is worn, clogged, or sitting incorrectly in the pit.

Old or Weak Pump

Older pumps can lose performance over time and may fail when you need them most.

No Backup Protection

A power outage during a storm can stop a standard sump pump. A backup system adds extra protection.
