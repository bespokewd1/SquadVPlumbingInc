A dishwasher that will not drain can look like an appliance problem, but the cause is sometimes in the plumbing around the kitchen sink. Food debris, a blocked filter, a kinked hose, or a slow kitchen drain can leave dirty water sitting in the bottom of the dishwasher.

## First, check if it is normal

Some dishwashers leave a small amount of water after a cycle. Before taking anything apart, check your owner's manual. If the manual says a little water is normal, monitor it and look for other symptoms such as odour, slow draining, leaking, or water backing into the sink.

If the water level is higher than usual or dirty water stays after every cycle, keep troubleshooting.

## Do not pour drain cleaner into the dishwasher

Do not use drain cleaner, dish soap, or the wrong detergent in a dishwasher. These can damage parts, create foam problems, or make the issue worse. If you are unsure, stop and call a professional.

## Simple plumbing checks for a dishwasher that will not drain

### 1. Clean the dishwasher filter

Most dishwashers have a filter near the bottom of the tub. Food scraps, glass, labels, and small debris can block it. Remove loose debris and clean the filter based on the owner's manual.

If the filter is damaged or the appliance still will not drain, the issue may be deeper in the hose, pump, or kitchen drain connection.

### 2. Check the kitchen sink drain

If your kitchen sink is slow, gurgling, or backing up, your dishwasher may struggle to drain too. The dishwasher often drains through the same kitchen plumbing path. A partial clog under the sink can affect both.

For Edmonton kitchen drain issues, Squad V can inspect the plumbing side, clear accessible blockages, repair leaks, and confirm whether the issue is in the drain line rather than the dishwasher.

### 3. Look for a kinked or pinched drain hose

The dishwasher drain hose can get kinked or pinched behind the appliance or under the sink. If the hose cannot carry water out, drainage slows or stops.

Only check areas you can reach safely. Turn off power before working near the dishwasher. If the hose is hidden, damaged, or hard to access, do not pull the dishwasher out by force. You can damage flooring, wiring, or water connections.

### 4. Check for leaks under the sink

Look under the sink for moisture, loose fittings, or signs of water staining. A leak near the dishwasher connection or kitchen drain piping needs attention before it damages cabinets or flooring.

Small leaks can turn into bigger plumbing repairs. If you see water, shut off the fixture if needed and call a plumber.

Good reasons to call include:

## How to prevent dishwasher drain problems

Scrape food into the garbage before loading dishes. Clean the filter regularly. Run hot water at the sink before starting a cycle if your dishwasher manual recommends it. Watch for slow kitchen drains, because they often show up before a full blockage.

During kitchen renovations, make sure the dishwasher drain hose, sink drain, water line, and shutoff access are planned before cabinets and countertops are installed. It is easier and cleaner to fix plumbing layout before the finish work is done.
