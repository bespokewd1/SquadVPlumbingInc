## What is a tankless water heater?

A tankless water heater heats water only when you need it. When you turn on a hot water tap, cold water moves through the unit. The burner starts, the heat exchanger warms the water, and hot water travels to your shower, sink, laundry, or dishwasher.

When you turn the tap off, the unit stops heating. That means the system is not keeping a full tank of water hot all day.

## Main benefits of tankless water heaters

### 1. Hot water when you need it

A correctly sized tankless water heater can provide steady hot water for daily household use. Instead of waiting for a tank to refill and reheat, the unit heats water as it flows through. This is helpful for busy Edmonton homes where showers, laundry, and dishwashing often happen close together.

Sizing still matters. Incoming water temperature, number of fixtures, gas supply, and household usage all affect performance. That is why a professional tankless water heater installation in Edmonton should start with proper sizing.

### 2. Less wasted energy

Traditional tanks use energy to keep stored water hot, even when no one is using hot water. Tankless units heat water on demand, which can reduce standby energy waste. Rinnai also notes that many tankless models are rated for high efficiency using UEF, or Uniform Energy Factor.

For homeowners comparing water heater replacement options, this can make tankless a smart long-term upgrade.

### 3. Compact design

Tankless units are much smaller than many storage tanks. Rinnai describes them as about the size of a carry-on suitcase. That compact size can help free up room in mechanical rooms, utility spaces, closets, or basements.

In Edmonton renovations, this can be useful when homeowners want more usable space or need a cleaner mechanical layout.

### 4. Repairable and serviceable

Tankless systems are built differently from storage tanks. Many parts can be serviced or replaced, which may help extend the usable life of the system when maintenance is done properly. Rinnai also notes that many tankless systems include strong warranty coverage.

For Edmonton tankless water heater repair or maintenance, use a trained technician who understands venting, gas supply, water quality, and manufacturer requirements.

### 5. Smart controls and convenience options

Some Rinnai tankless water heaters can connect with Wi-Fi controls, scheduling, recirculation settings, and other smart features. Rinnai also highlights accessories such as push-button demand and motion sensor options that can help make hot water available faster.

Recirculation can reduce wait time at fixtures. In some homes, Rinnai technology can support this even without a dedicated return line. A site visit is the best way to confirm which setup fits your plumbing.

### 6. Natural gas and propane options

Tankless water heaters can work with natural gas or propane, depending on the model and property setup. Some all-electric homes may still have options, but those need a closer review by a qualified installer.

In Edmonton, proper venting, gas line capacity, condensate handling, and code-compliant installation are important. Do not size or install a gas tankless unit by guesswork.

## Is tankless better than a hot water tank?

Tankless can be better when you want:

- More consistent hot water for busy household routines
- Better energy efficiency than many traditional gas tanks
- More space in your mechanical room
- A serviceable system with replaceable parts
- Smart controls or recirculation features
- A long-term water heater upgrade for your home

A traditional tank may still be the right choice for some homes, budgets, or plumbing layouts. Best answer depends on hot water demand, existing equipment, gas line, venting path, and installation cost.

## Tankless water heater FAQ

### Do tankless water heaters run out of hot water?

A properly sized tankless unit heats water as it is used, so it does not run out the same way a storage tank can. However, every unit has a flow rate limit. If too many fixtures run at the same time, performance can drop. Correct sizing is important.

### Can a tankless water heater save energy?

Tankless systems can reduce standby energy waste because they do not keep a full tank hot all day. High-efficiency models can improve savings further, depending on usage, model, installation, and energy prices.

### Are tankless water heaters good for Edmonton homes?

Yes, they can be a strong option for many Edmonton homes. Cold incoming water temperature makes sizing important, so a local installer should calculate demand before recommending a model.
