A renovation can make your home work better, but plumbing decisions need to happen before walls close and finishes go in. A good layout helps prevent leaks, awkward fixture placement, low access for repairs, and costly changes later.

Squad V Plumbing Inc. provides renovation plumbing in Edmonton for bathrooms, kitchens, basements, fixture replacements, rough-ins, water heater work, and gasfitting. Use these tips before starting your next project.

## Start with the plumbing plan

Before demolition, decide what is staying, what is moving, and what needs to be replaced. Moving a toilet, shower, tub, vanity, kitchen sink, dishwasher connection, or laundry line can add cost because it may require new water lines, drains, venting, framing changes, and inspections.

If the current layout works, keeping fixtures close to their existing locations can help control the budget. If the layout does not work, plan the plumbing move early so it can be done safely and cleanly.

## Check old plumbing before new finishes

Renovations are a good time to inspect supply lines, shutoff valves, drains, traps, and visible piping. If there is corrosion, staining, poor slope, old valves, or signs of past leaks, fix them before new tile, cabinets, or drywall cover the area.

Repairing hidden plumbing after a finished renovation is much more disruptive than handling it during rough-in.

## Plan bathroom fixtures around access and drainage

For bathroom renovations, think beyond the look of the fixtures. Toilets, tubs, showers, and vanities need correct drain location, venting, water supply, slope, and service access.

## Plan kitchen plumbing before cabinets arrive

Kitchen plumbing should be confirmed before cabinets, countertops, and appliances are installed. Sink location, faucet style, dishwasher connection, shutoff access, and drain route should all match the renovation plan.

If you are updating a kitchen in Edmonton, Squad V can help with kitchen plumbing upgrades, fixture replacement, leak repairs, and rough-in work tied to the renovation.

## Include water heater planning

Renovations can change hot water demand. A new bathroom, larger shower, basement suite-style layout, or upgraded fixtures can put more demand on an old water heater.

Before finishing the project, ask whether your hot water tank or tankless water heater is properly sized and accessible for maintenance. Squad V installs and services hot water tanks and tankless water heaters, including Rinnai tankless options.

## Do not forget gasfitting needs

Some renovations involve gas appliances, furnace changes, or gas line work. Gasfitting needs a certified technician and proper code-compliant installation. Do not move or modify gas lines during DIY renovation work.

Squad V provides certified Edmonton gasfitting for gas line installation, repairs, appliance hookups, safety inspections, and maintenance.

## Renovation plumbing checklist

Before work begins, confirm:

- Which fixtures stay and which fixtures move
- Whether drains and vents need changes
- Where shutoff valves will be accessible
- Whether old piping or valves should be replaced
- Whether hot water demand will increase
- Whether gas line work is part of the project
- Whether plumbing rough-in should happen before framing or drywall

Good planning reduces surprises. It also helps builders, homeowners, and plumbers stay aligned during the job.
